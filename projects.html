NextGen eSports collectibles

Language: German
2021 (Vienna - AT)
Chancen und Herausforderungen beim Handel mit NFTs im Zusammenhang mit eSports.

In my paper, I explored the dynamic relationship between NFTs and the eSports scene, honing in on the NBA's ventures into this digital realm.
While unraveling the legal aspects, I aimed to decipher the impact of these unique digital tokens on the gaming industry. By delving into the specifics of NBA NFTs, I sought to strike a balance, shedding light on both the exciting possibilities and potential legal challenges in this evolving landscape where gaming, digital collectibles, and the law intersect.

In vino veritas - Wein im alten Ägypten

Language: German
Publication in: Nilus 25. Bernhard Palme, Angelika Zdiarsky (eds.). Vienna 2019.
Study of 3 papyri

Translation and analysis of 3 papyri regarding lease agreements, employment and purchase contracts, all centering around the subject of viticulture in ancient egypt.
The chronological dating of the papyri ranges from the 2nd to the 5th century A.D.

Das asylrecht des Proxenos

Language: German
2017 (Vienna - AT)
14 Pages

An analysis of the ancient greek institution called "proxenía" which marks the beginning of the modern institution of the ambassador.
From its earliest form of hereditary friendship and alliance in prehomeric greece, to its advanced stage as an official institution of the poleis, the proxenía included an immesurable variety of different responsibilities, authorities and forms of jurisdiction, few more interesting and of current significance, than the right to grant asylum.
As publications on this particular subject are more than scarse, the primary source consists nearly exclusively of a vast number of inscriptions from different periods and realities of the ancient greek world.

"Fanes" -Introduction and analysis of the ladin epos

Language: Italian
2016 (Bolzano - IT)
26 Pages

A brief introduction to the ancient ladin tale of "Fanes", based on the collection of K.F.WOLFF (Dolomitensagen), followed by a deeper inspection on why it should rather be regarded as an epos than as a myth.
Subsequently the epos is analyzed on its archetypal components, which are then compared to those of the greek and norse epic circles.
